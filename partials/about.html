Liliana Carvalho is a Portuguese architect and Sound Artist.

Her Diploma in Architecture got her to study and work in Poland, Portugal, Germany and UK, where she is currently based [London].

Whilst studying she collaborated for Ru( as radio technician and broadcaster, which became her first hands-on experience with electronic music. It didn't took her to long to start as guest DJ and manage events with the Portuguese techno promoter and label Soniculture; also engaging later on with the production of Forte Festival ed.2014.

The co-founding and co-managing of SonicLab’s early stages was the milestone for forthcoming sonic experimental projects, starting with XNX installation premiered at Semibreve Festival '14.

Exploring for new grounds Architecture she moved to London, where she decided to also study Sound Art. This was not only crucial and influential on her sonic work, but also a determinant step for her inherent expression of continuous reciprocity on Sound and Space narrative.

approach

I am interested in developing strategies or systems that combine my architectural knowledge with a sonic experience: exploring responsive and critical design that draws strongly from Space to Sound and vice-versa.

How can Architecture compose and mediate a sonic awareness? How can Sound Art shape and orchestrate a spatial idea?

By intertwining theory, research and practice on both these fields with an aural and phenomenological approach, my works result in a continuous expression of this reciprocity narrative of Sound and Space.

Due to the cross-pollination nature of the production process I believe collaboration projects work as an essential tool of investigation, learning and creation. The resulting reflections have been shaped in sound compositions, installations, performances, structures and systems.
